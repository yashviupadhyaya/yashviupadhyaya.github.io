Me & HCI...


During my undergrad in Information Technology, I gave a technical presentation as a part of an academic course - ‘Principles of Communication’ on the electric wheelchair of Stephen Hawking, proposed by the Human-Computer Interaction team from the Intel labs. The presentation gave me a holistic understanding of how technologies can be developed to overcome the problems that exist. In the quest for the answer to my ever daunting question - “ How do I make a useful project? ” I got the answer … It's the ‘Users’ who form the true foundation of usable technologies. I instantly knew which area I want to work on... It was the technology that interacts with the users... It was Human-Computer Interaction which answered my question regarding the usability of a product.

What I think….


In my view, the world we live in has abundant real-world challenges and excessive systems. Building accessible technologies can bridge the gap between human needs and technological solutions. This can be done using modern techniques in the field of Human-Computer Interaction. My goal is to develop healthcare products for the disabled to aid them in communicating and performing daily tasks.
